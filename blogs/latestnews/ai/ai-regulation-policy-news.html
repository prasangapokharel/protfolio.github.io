AI Regulation Roundup: The Global Race to Govern Tech

February 1, 2024 • 7 min read • Policy & Law

The Brussels Effect

The European Union has taken the lead with the landmark EU AI Act. This risk-based framework categorizes AI systems by their potential for harm, banning unacceptable risks (like social scoring) and imposing strict transparency requirements on high-risk applications. This legislation is likely to set the global standard, much like GDPR did for privacy.

The US Approach: Innovation vs. Safety

In contrast to the EU's comprehensive legislation, the United States is relying on Executive Orders and agency-specific guidelines. The focus remains on maintaining American technological leadership while establishing voluntary safety commitments from major AI labs like OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic.

International Cooperation

Recognizing that AI knows no borders, nations are coming together through initiatives like the Bletchley Declaration. Global summits are becoming regular occurrences as leaders attempt to align on safety standards for "Frontier AI" models before they surpass human control.
