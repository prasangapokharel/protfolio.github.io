The Great Migration: Why Governments are Moving to Sovereign Clouds

February 1, 2026 • 8 min read • Cloud Infrastructure

Data Sovereignty is National Security

In the early 2020s, the "Public Cloud" (AWS, Azure, Google) was the default destination for government IT. In 2026, the tide has turned. Geopolitical tensions and stringent regulations like the EU's Data Act have made "Sovereign Cloud" the top priority for CIOs in the public sector.

Sovereign Cloud ensures that all data, including metadata, stays within national borders and is subject only to the laws of that country. It protects against foreign subpoenas (like the US CLOUD Act) and ensures continuity of government services even during international conflicts.

Key Drivers of Migration

  • Legal Compliance: Meeting strict data residency requirements for health, tax, and census data.
  • Operational Independence: Avoiding vendor lock-in with foreign tech giants by using local partners or hybrid models.
  • Cyber Resilience: Reducing the attack surface by physically isolating critical infrastructure from the public internet.

The Hybrid Reality

This doesn't mean the end of public cloud use. Instead, governments are adopting a tiered approach. "Secret" data stays on air-gapped Sovereign Clouds, while public-facing websites and non-sensitive workloads leverage the scalability of global hyperscalers. This hybrid model offers the best of both worlds: security where needed, and efficiency where possible.
